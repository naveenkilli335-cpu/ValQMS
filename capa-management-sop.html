1.0 PURPOSE

The purpose of this Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is to establish a systematic and effective process for identifying, investigating, implementing, and monitoring Corrective Actions (CA) and Preventive Actions (PA) to address actual and potential quality issues. This procedure ensures that CAPAs are managed in accordance with FDA 21 CFR Part 211, ICH Q10, EU GMP, ISO 9001:2015, and ISO 13485:2016 requirements to maintain product quality, patient safety, and continuous improvement.

Key Objectives:
  • Eliminate the causes of existing nonconformities to prevent recurrence
  • Identify and address potential nonconformities before they occur
  • Ensure systematic investigation and root cause analysis
  • Implement effective corrective and preventive measures
  • Verify effectiveness of implemented actions
  • Support continual improvement of the quality system
  • Meet regulatory requirements and inspection readiness

2.0 SCOPE

This SOP applies to all Corrective and Preventive Actions initiated within the pharmaceutical manufacturing facility and covers:

2.1 Sources Requiring CAPA

  • Deviations: Manufacturing, laboratory, and equipment deviations
  • Out of Specification (OOS): Analytical test results outside specifications
  • Out of Trend (OOT): Data trending showing adverse patterns
  • Complaints: Customer complaints affecting product quality or patient safety
  • Product Recalls: Field actions and market withdrawals
  • Failed Batches: Rejected or discarded production batches
  • Audit Findings: Internal, supplier, customer, and regulatory audit observations
  • Inspection Observations: FDA, EMA, and other regulatory findings
  • Change Control: Issues identified during change implementation
  • Validation Failures: Process, cleaning, or analytical method validation issues
  • Equipment Failures: Critical equipment malfunctions
  • Environmental Monitoring: Cleanroom excursions and contamination events
  • Data Integrity Issues: Audit trail anomalies and data discrepancies
  • Management Review: Issues identified during quality system reviews

2.2 Exclusions

Minor editorial corrections to documents that do not affect product quality, safety, or GMP compliance do not require CAPA. These are handled through the Document Control procedure (SOP-DOC-002).

2.3 Integration with Quality System

The CAPA system is integrated with and supports:

  • Quality Risk Management (SOP-QMS-002)
  • Change Control Management (SOP-QMS-001)
  • Deviation Management (SOP-QMS-004)
  • Complaint Management (SOP-QMS-005)
  • Management Review (SOP-QMS-006)
