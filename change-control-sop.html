1.0 PURPOSE

The purpose of this Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is to establish a systematic and controlled process for initiating, reviewing, approving, implementing, and documenting changes to pharmaceutical manufacturing processes, equipment, facilities, systems, procedures, and documentation. This procedure ensures that all changes are properly evaluated for their impact on product quality, safety, efficacy, regulatory compliance, and validation status in accordance with FDA 21 CFR Part 211, ICH Q10, EU GMP, and ISO 13485 requirements.

Key Objectives:
  • Ensure systematic evaluation of all proposed changes
  • Maintain product quality, safety, and efficacy
  • Ensure regulatory compliance and validation integrity
  • Facilitate continual improvement and innovation
  • Prevent unintended consequences from changes

2.0 SCOPE

This SOP applies to all changes that may impact product quality, safety, efficacy, regulatory compliance, or GMP requirements across the entire product lifecycle, including but not limited to:

  • Manufacturing Changes: Process parameters, manufacturing methods, batch sizes, production schedules
  • Equipment Changes: New equipment installation, modifications, calibration methods, cleaning procedures
  • Facility Changes: HVAC systems, utilities, cleanroom modifications, environmental monitoring
  • Material Changes: Raw materials, excipients, packaging components, suppliers, specifications
  • Document Changes: SOPs, batch records, specifications, analytical methods, validation protocols
  • System Changes: Computerized systems, automated equipment, LIMS, ERP systems
  • Organizational Changes: Key personnel, responsibilities, organizational structure
  • Analytical Changes: Test methods, acceptance criteria, laboratory equipment
This SOP does not apply to minor editorial corrections that do not affect procedural content or meaning (e.g., spelling corrections, formatting adjustments). Such changes may be handled as document revisions under SOP-DOC-002.

3.0 DEFINITIONS

Term Definition
Change Control A formal system by which qualified representatives of appropriate disciplines review proposed or actual changes that might affect a validated status. The intent is to determine the need for action to ensure and document that the system is maintained in a validated state.
Change Control Board (CCB) A cross-functional team responsible for reviewing and approving change requests. Typical members include Quality Assurance, Manufacturing, Engineering, Regulatory Affairs, and other relevant departments.
Change Request (CR) A formal documented proposal to modify a process, system, equipment, facility, document, or material that may impact product quality or GMP compliance.
Impact Assessment A systematic evaluation of the potential effects of a proposed change on product quality, safety, efficacy, validation status, regulatory compliance, and other relevant factors.
Critical Change A change that directly impacts product critical quality attributes (CQAs), safety, efficacy, or requires regulatory notification/approval.
Major Change A change that could potentially impact product quality or GMP compliance but does not directly affect CQAs. May require revalidation or qualification activities.
Minor Change A change with minimal or no impact on product quality or GMP compliance. Requires documentation but limited assessment.

4.0 RESPONSIBILITIES

4.1 Change Initiator

  • Identify and document the need for change using the Change Request Form (Appendix A)
  • Provide complete justification and rationale for the proposed change
  • Collaborate with relevant departments during impact assessment
  • Implement approved changes according to the implementation plan
  • Complete post-implementation verification activities

4.2 Department Head / Manager

  • Review change requests initiated by department personnel
  • Ensure adequate resources are available for change implementation
  • Provide technical expertise during impact assessment
  • Approve departmental implementation activities
  • Ensure affected personnel receive appropriate training

4.3 Quality Assurance (QA)

  • Coordinate the change control process and maintain the change control system
  • Review all change requests for completeness and GMP impact
  • Lead or participate in impact assessments and risk evaluations
  • Ensure validation/qualification requirements are identified and addressed
  • Verify implementation completion and effectiveness
  • Maintain change control documentation and trending analysis
  • Final approval authority for all changes

4.4 Change Control Board (CCB)

The CCB is responsible for reviewing and approving changes based on classification:

Change Type CCB Membership Meeting Frequency
Critical VP Quality, Head of Manufacturing, Head of Engineering, Head of R&D, Regulatory Affairs Manager, QA Manager As needed, within 5 business days
Major Head of Quality, Manufacturing Manager, Engineering Manager, QA Manager, Relevant Department Heads Weekly or as needed
Minor QA Manager, Relevant Department Manager As submitted

CCB Responsibilities:

  • Review change requests and supporting documentation
  • Evaluate impact assessments and risk analyses
  • Determine validation/qualification requirements
  • Approve or reject change requests with documented rationale
  • Establish timelines for change implementation
  • Review post-implementation effectiveness

4.5 Regulatory Affairs

  • Evaluate regulatory impact of proposed changes
  • Determine if regulatory notifications or submissions are required
  • Prepare and submit regulatory filings (annual reports, prior approval supplements, CBE submissions)
  • Maintain regulatory change log

4.6 Validation Team

  • Assess impact on validated status
  • Determine if revalidation, requalification, or extended validation is required
  • Develop and execute validation protocols when needed
  • Review validation data and approve validation reports

6.0 CHANGE CONTROL PROCESS

The change control process consists of the following phases:

6.1 Phase 1: Initiation

  1. Identify Need for Change: Any employee may identify the need for a change based on observations, investigations, process improvements, or regulatory updates.
  2. Complete Change Request Form (Appendix A):
    • Unique Change Control number (auto-generated by QMS system)
    • Detailed description of proposed change
    • Justification and business rationale
    • Affected areas, products, and systems
    • Proposed implementation date
    • Preliminary classification (subject to QA review)
  3. Submit to Department Manager: The change initiator submits the CR to their department manager for initial review and endorsement.
  4. Forward to QA: Once endorsed, the CR is forwarded to Quality Assurance for formal acceptance and classification.

6.2 Phase 2: Assessment and Evaluation

  1. QA Initial Review (within 2 business days):
    • Verify completeness of Change Request
    • Confirm or revise classification
    • Assign to appropriate CCB level
    • Identify assessment team members
  2. Impact Assessment (Appendix B): Cross-functional team evaluates:
    • Product Impact: Effect on critical quality attributes, specifications, stability
    • Process Impact: Changes to manufacturing parameters, procedures, controls
    • Equipment/Facility Impact: Qualification status, cleaning, maintenance
    • Documentation Impact: SOPs, batch records, specifications requiring updates
    • Validation Impact: Need for revalidation, qualification, or extended studies
    • Regulatory Impact: Filing requirements, notifications, commitments
    • Supply Chain Impact: Material availability, lead times, suppliers
    • Training Impact: Personnel requiring training on new procedures
  3. Risk Assessment (Section 7.0): Perform quality risk management evaluation using ICH Q9 principles.
  4. Develop Implementation Plan: For approved changes, create detailed plan including:
    • Required activities and timelines
    • Validation/qualification protocols if needed
    • Document updates required
    • Training requirements
    • Resource allocation
    • Verification/acceptance criteria

6.3 Phase 3: Review and Approval

  1. CCB Review Meeting:
    • Present change request, impact assessment, and risk analysis
    • Discuss implementation plan and resource requirements
    • Address questions and concerns from CCB members
  2. CCB Decision: The CCB will:
    • Approve: Change proceeds to implementation
    • Approve with Conditions: Specific requirements must be met
    • Defer: Additional information or assessment needed
    • Reject: Change not approved with documented rationale
  3. Final QA Approval: Head of Quality or VP Quality provides final written approval based on change classification.
