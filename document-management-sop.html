1.0 PURPOSE

The purpose of this Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is to define the standardized process for creation, review, approval, issuance, distribution, revision, periodic review, and archival of controlled documents within the Quality Management System (QMS). It ensures that current, approved documents are available at points of use; obsolete documents are removed promptly; and electronic records and signatures meet applicable regulatory requirements including 21 CFR Part 11, EU GMP Chapter 4, and Annex 11.

Key Objectives:
  • Ensure only approved, current documents are in use
  • Maintain document traceability, version control, and integrity
  • Ensure Part 11 compliant controls for electronic records and signatures
  • Enable timely document distribution and withdrawal
  • Ensure training completion prior to document effective date

2.0 SCOPE

This SOP applies to all controlled documents within the QMS, including but not limited to: SOPs, Policies, Work Instructions (WIs), Forms/Templates, Specifications, Quality Manuals, Batch/Testing Record templates, Drawings, and Electronic Controlled Documents managed in the electronic Document Management System (eDMS). It covers both paper and electronic formats.

  • In Scope: Creation, revision, approval, issuance, training, distribution, periodic review, obsolescence, archival, and retrieval of controlled documents; management of external standards copied into the QMS; control of printed copies.
  • Out of Scope: Uncontrolled reference materials, personal notes, transient whiteboard content. Records generated by processes are governed by Control of Records SOP.

3.0 DEFINITIONS

Term Definition
Controlled Document A document managed under formal control ensuring unique identification, versioning, approval, effective date, distribution, and change history (e.g., SOPs, WIs, forms).
Uncontrolled Copy A printed or exported copy not subject to update control; marked ‚ÄúUNCONTROLLED COPY‚Äù. For reference only.
Document Owner Individual responsible for document content accuracy, periodic review, and initiating revisions.
eDMS Electronic Document Management System used to manage controlled documents and related workflows.
Electronic Record Any combination of text, graphics, data, audio, or other information in digital form created, modified, maintained, archived, retrieved, or distributed by a computer system.
Electronic Signature A computer data compilation of any symbol or series of symbols executed, adopted, or authorized by an individual to be the legally binding equivalent of the individual's handwritten signature (per 21 CFR Part 11).
Read & Understand (R&U) Training acknowledgement that personnel have reviewed and understood a document prior to its effective use.
Obsolete Document A document withdrawn from use and retained only for historical/reference purposes with appropriate labeling and access control.

4.0 RESPONSIBILITIES

4.1 Document Owner

  • Draft new documents and revisions with accurate, clear content
  • Ensure technical correctness and alignment with current practices
  • Perform and document periodic reviews within defined intervals
  • Initiate revisions and coordinate stakeholder review

4.2 Approvers

  • Independently review content for adequacy and compliance
  • Approve via electronic signatures in the eDMS
  • Ensure impacted areas are trained prior to effective date

4.3 QA Document Control

  • Administer the eDMS and maintain master documents
  • Verify proper numbering, metadata, versioning, and watermarking
  • Ensure distribution, withdrawal, and archival controls are followed
  • Maintain audit trails and ensure Part 11 controls are enforced

4.4 System Administrator (IT/CSV)

  • Maintain access controls, backups, and disaster recovery for the eDMS
  • Ensure system validation and change control per Annex 11/Part 11
  • Support audit trail retention and retrieval

4.5 End Users

  • Use only current, approved documents available in the eDMS
  • Complete assigned Read & Understand training before effective date
  • Report identified errors or improvement needs to Document Owner

5.0 DOCUMENT TYPES & NUMBERING

All controlled documents shall be uniquely identified and numbered according to a standardized schema to ensure traceability and retrieval.

5.1 Document Types

5.2 Numbering & Versioning

  • Numbers are assigned by QA Document Control; numbers are not reused.
  • Version format: Major.Minor (e.g., 1.0, 1.1). Major increments for content changes; minor for editorial.
  • Each document includes Title, Number, Version, Effective Date, Owner, Approvers, and controlled footer.

6.0 CHANGE CONTROL PROCESS

The change control process consists of the following phases:

6.1 Phase 1: Initiation

  1. Identify Need for Change: Any employee may identify the need for a change based on observations, investigations, process improvements, or regulatory updates.
  2. Complete Change Request Form (Appendix A):
    • Unique Change Control number (auto-generated by QMS system)
    • Detailed description of proposed change
    • Justification and business rationale
    • Affected areas, products, and systems
    • Proposed implementation date
    • Preliminary classification (subject to QA review)
  3. Submit to Department Manager: The change initiator submits the CR to their department manager for initial review and endorsement.
  4. Forward to QA: Once endorsed, the CR is forwarded to Quality Assurance for formal acceptance and classification.

6.2 Phase 2: Assessment and Evaluation

  1. QA Initial Review (within 2 business days):
    • Verify completeness of Change Request
    • Confirm or revise classification
    • Assign to appropriate CCB level
    • Identify assessment team members
  2. Impact Assessment (Appendix B): Cross-functional team evaluates:
    • Product Impact: Effect on critical quality attributes, specifications, stability
    • Process Impact: Changes to manufacturing parameters, procedures, controls
    • Equipment/Facility Impact: Qualification status, cleaning, maintenance
    • Documentation Impact: SOPs, batch records, specifications requiring updates
    • Validation Impact: Need for revalidation, qualification, or extended studies
    • Regulatory Impact: Filing requirements, notifications, commitments
    • Supply Chain Impact: Material availability, lead times, suppliers
    • Training Impact: Personnel requiring training on new procedures
  3. Risk Assessment (Section 7.0): Perform quality risk management evaluation using ICH Q9 principles.
  4. Develop Implementation Plan: For approved changes, create detailed plan including:
    • Required activities and timelines
    • Validation/qualification protocols if needed
    • Document updates required
    • Training requirements
    • Resource allocation
    • Verification/acceptance criteria

6.3 Phase 3: Review and Approval

  1. CCB Review Meeting:
    • Present change request, impact assessment, and risk analysis
    • Discuss implementation plan and resource requirements
    • Address questions and concerns from CCB members
  2. CCB Decision: The CCB will:
    • Approve: Change proceeds to implementation
    • Approve with Conditions: Specific requirements must be met
    • Defer: Additional information or assessment needed
    • Reject: Change not approved with documented rationale
  3. Final QA Approval: Head of Quality or VP Quality provides final written approval based on change classification.
