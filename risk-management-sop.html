1.0 PURPOSE

The purpose of this Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is to establish a systematic approach to Quality Risk Management (QRM) that is proactive, consistent with ICH Q9(R1) principles, and integrated throughout the product lifecycle. This procedure ensures that quality risk management is applied to identify, assess, control, communicate, and review risks to product quality, patient safety, and data integrity in accordance with ICH Q9(R1), FDA guidelines, EU GMP Annex 20, and ISO 31000 requirements.

Key Objectives:
  • Provide a systematic approach to quality risk management
  • Enable proactive identification and control of potential risks
  • Support science-based and risk-based decision making
  • Ensure patient safety and product quality
  • Facilitate regulatory compliance and inspection readiness
  • Support continual improvement through risk review

2.0 SCOPE

This SOP applies to all quality risk management activities throughout the product lifecycle at our pharmaceutical manufacturing facility, including but not limited to:

  • Development: Formulation development, process design, scale-up activities
  • Manufacturing: Facility design, equipment qualification, process validation, routine production
  • Technology Transfer: Site changes, process transfers, outsourcing activities
  • Change Control: Assessment of changes to materials, processes, equipment, facilities
  • Deviation Management: Risk assessment of deviations and investigations
  • CAPA: Risk-based prioritization and effectiveness monitoring
  • Supplier Management: Supplier qualification and monitoring
  • Computerized Systems: Data integrity risks, system validation
  • Cleaning Validation: Cross-contamination risk assessment
  • Stability Studies: Risk-based stability programs

3.0 DEFINITIONS

Term Definition (ICH Q9(R1))
Risk The combination of the probability of occurrence of harm and the severity of that harm. In the context of pharmaceutical quality, risk is typically related to impacts on product quality, patient safety, or data integrity.
Quality Risk Management A systematic process for the assessment, control, communication, and review of risks to the quality of the medicinal product across the product lifecycle.
Risk Assessment A systematic process of organizing information to support a risk decision. It consists of risk identification, risk analysis, and risk evaluation.
Risk Identification The systematic use of information to identify potential sources of harm (hazards) referring to the risk question or problem description.
Risk Analysis The estimation of the risk associated with the identified hazards. It is the qualitative or quantitative process of linking the likelihood of occurrence and severity of harms.
Risk Evaluation The comparison of the estimated risk against given risk criteria using a quantitative or qualitative scale to determine the significance of the risk.
Risk Control Actions implementing risk management decisions. Risk control includes risk reduction and risk acceptance.
Risk Communication The sharing of information about risk and risk management between the decision makers and others.
Risk Review Review or monitoring of output/results of the risk management process considering (if appropriate) new knowledge and experience about the risk.
Formality The level of rigor, detail, and documentation applied to a risk management activity, scaled to the level of risk, complexity, and importance.
Detectability The ability to discover or determine the existence, presence, or fact of a hazard. Also referred to as "detection" in some risk tools.

4.0 RESPONSIBILITIES

4.1 Senior Management

  • Provide leadership and resources for effective quality risk management
  • Ensure quality risk management is integrated into the Pharmaceutical Quality System
  • Review significant risk assessments and approve risk acceptance decisions
  • Promote a culture that supports proactive risk identification and management
  • Approve risk management policies and procedures

4.2 Quality Assurance (QA)

  • Oversee the implementation of quality risk management processes
  • Provide guidance on appropriate risk management tools and methodologies
  • Review and approve risk assessments for critical activities
  • Ensure risk management documentation meets regulatory requirements
  • Coordinate risk communication activities
  • Monitor effectiveness of risk control measures
  • Schedule and facilitate periodic risk reviews

4.3 Risk Management Team Leader

  • Facilitate risk assessment workshops and meetings
  • Ensure appropriate cross-functional team participation
  • Guide the team through selected risk management tools
  • Ensure risk assessment outputs are documented appropriately
  • Follow up on implementation of risk control measures
  • Report risk assessment results to stakeholders

4.4 Risk Management Team Members

  • Participate actively in risk assessment activities
  • Provide technical expertise and subject matter knowledge
  • Identify potential risks based on their area of expertise
  • Propose and implement risk control measures
  • Document risk information within their functional area
  • Report changes that may impact previous risk assessments

4.5 Subject Matter Experts (SMEs)

  • Provide specialized technical knowledge for risk assessments
  • Support risk analysis with data and scientific rationale
  • Recommend appropriate risk control strategies
  • Review and validate risk assessment conclusions

4.6 Process/Product Owners

  • Ensure risk assessments are performed for their processes/products
  • Allocate resources for risk management activities
  • Implement approved risk control measures
  • Monitor effectiveness of risk controls
  • Initiate risk reviews when changes occur

4.7 Regulatory Affairs

  • Provide guidance on regulatory expectations for risk management
  • Review risk assessments that may impact regulatory submissions
  • Support communication of risk information to regulatory authorities
  • Monitor regulatory guidance updates related to risk management
Note on Cross-Functional Teams: Risk management activities should involve appropriate cross-functional teams with diverse expertise. Team composition should be scaled to the complexity and criticality of the risk being assessed, in alignment with ICH Q9(R1) principles of formality.

5.0 ICH Q9(R1) PRINCIPLES

Quality risk management is based on the following key principles as outlined in ICH Q9(R1):

5.1 Core Principles

Principle Description
Patient Safety First The evaluation of risk to quality should ultimately be based on patient protection. Risk management decisions should prioritize patient safety above all other considerations.
Science-Based Risk assessments should be based on scientific knowledge, process understanding, and experience. Decisions should be supported by data and technical rationale.
Effort Scaled to Risk The level of formality, documentation, and resources applied to quality risk management should be commensurate with the level of risk.
Product Lifecycle Approach Quality risk management should be integrated throughout the product lifecycle, from development through discontinuation.
Proactive Approach Risk management should be proactive, identifying and addressing potential problems before they occur, rather than only reacting to issues.

5.3 Risk-Based Decision Making

Quality risk management supports risk-based decision making by:

  • Providing a structured framework for evaluating quality impacts
  • Enabling prioritization of activities based on risk significance
  • Supporting justification for decisions with scientific rationale
  • Facilitating communication with regulatory authorities
  • Optimizing resource allocation to address highest risks
All risk management activities must maintain focus on patient protection as the primary consideration. Economic factors should not override patient safety considerations in risk-based decisions.
